01 — About

Physics meets machine intelligence

I'm a Physics postgraduate student at Bhaktavatsalam Memorial College for Women, Chennai, working at the intersection of physical simulation, machine learning, and scientific computing.

My research focuses on making AI systems that genuinely understand physics — not just pattern-match on data, but reason with physical constraints, symmetries, and governing equations.

From implementing Fourier Neural Operators for PDE solving to building Bayesian hierarchical models for 6100+ exoplanet systems, I work where rigorous physics meets scalable computation.

I'm especially drawn to self-supervised and unsupervised learning on scientific instrumentation data — time-series, photometry, and imaging — and to agentic AI systems for automated scientific discovery, alongside continued work on neural operators for PDE solving.
